My Projects

Painting Station

One of the steps involved in the production of walking is to stain and spray the wood,
this lead to creating a system that allows the three part wood to spin while changing
the speed as desired, detect if the door is open and set the fan for drying, while detecting desired
temperature in the system to determine if the fan is be switched off or on.
Using Programmable Logic Controllers (PLC) I was able to implement these function.
As seen in the picture on the right many alterations, implementations and testing of
different devices such as initially using a stepper motor and switching to
DC motor for better heat resistance, adding relays to control the ON/OFF of the motors.
For a better interaction of the machine a C-more EA9 series HMI is
used to control the speed and switch the system Off/On















Water Filter System

One of the projects worked in the learning factory at McMaster University is the
water filter system. Using activated charcoal(charcoal soaked in 25% salt water solution),
to remove particles and chemicals present in water and an ultrafiltration which eliminates
any pathogens that is virus and bacteria present in the water. In order to reduce cost and
enhance flexibility, a water filter was designed and 3D printed. The process of this system as
shown by the picture on the right is, water goes into the filter, through activated charcoal,
through cotton to remove charcoal residue and through a mesh. The outlet of the
filter goes through an ultrafiltration (UV light), which goes through a valve.
In order to monitor this system and ensure proper filtration, turbidity sensor was added
before filtration and after filtration. A UV sensor was placed beside the UV light to
ensure it is ON when operating, and if not so the valve would close preventing users from
consuming the water.














Dispensing Station

In production of walking cane in the learning factory at McMaster University, on of the steps
taken is to glue brass couplers to the cane. In the process of doing so, there was an inconsistent
amount of glue which has lead damage to some wooden cane parts, with some cane not strong enough
due little adhesive dispensed into the cane parts. In order to eliminate this problem, a dispensing station
was integrated. With a set amount of time programmed with PLC and displayed on thin client for users to interact and produce, the dispensing station eliminates the issue of inconsistent use of adhesive in production and aids to maintain quality.



















Walking Cane

A walking cane is as old as time but very little technology are implemented in it. Working with the learning factory, I started developing ways to make a cane smart. The first step to this was understanding the production of a regular cane, I made 3D CAD models of prototype, added pressure sensor to the cane to read values of how much force an individual is putting on the cane this will allow to know if someone’s health condition is getting better or worse. Constant development are made to produce a better product of cane hence a club under McMaster Bachelor of Technology Association was formed. This allowed for sale of the product and continuity of production of the smart walking cane.
